📖 Factor Guide & Capacity Simulator
Purpose: This page helps you identify what limits your warehouse throughput across three main functions — Inbound (IB), Inventory (INV), and Outbound (OB). The system bottleneck is whichever function has the lowest Max ADO (Average Daily Orders). To increase total throughput, the system bottleneck must be addressed first.

Step-level bottleneck: Within each function, capacity equals the minimum step capacity. IB has three steps: Arrival Staging → Receiving → Putaway. OB has four steps: Sorting → Packing → Pre-sorting → PO Staging. The weakest step is highlighted in red — improving it directly raises the function's overall capacity without needing to change any other step.

How to simulate: Each factor row has a Benchmark value (the theoretical design target, locked by default) and a Modified value (what you want to test). Change any Modified value and click ▶ Calculate. The sticky bar at the top always shows the Benchmark result and, after calculating, the Modified result with Δ vs Benchmark.

Space balancing: Each step has a Space Allocation row showing the SQM assigned to it. The number of stations or pallets is derived from SQM ÷ unit size. If you change SQM, a reminder will prompt you to update related station/pallet counts and consider adjusting other functions' SQM accordingly.
